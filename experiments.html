Experiment 1: AI Music Generation

Tool used: Creative Fabrica Studio AI

Aim: I wanted to test whether generative AI can create a convincing, atmospheric pop song which has artistic value.

Prompt used: Make an electronic pop song with male vocals.

Outcome: The AI succesfully generated a full song based on the prompt, which I then listened to and evaluated in terms of style, quality, and originality.

Reflection: The song that was produced, I have to say, was very impressive. It had a great beat, and the lyrics were decent. If I heard it on the radio, I would genuinely just think it was a regular song. This instils quite a bit of fear in me, because we are clearly at a stage where music generated from datasets can sound convincingly human, and this could leave artists vulnerable to having their ideas and originality outshone by these kinds of works.

Experiment 2: Writing a Song Within One Hour

Tool used: Logic Pro, Apple's Digital Audio Workstation which I have been using for 10+ years, and also a MIDI keyboard for the instruments

Aim: To compare the speed, quality, and artistic value of a song created under human time pressure with the output of generative AI.

Method: Within one hour, I tasked myself with creating a song from scratch. I tend to start by playing around with similar chords I already know on a piano software instrument, just to get a feeling for how I would like the song to progress. From there, I start humming a melody, which I then begin to pick out on my MIDI keyboard. Once I decide on a strong melodic progression, I start looking into what kinds of instruments, drums, and other elements can be added. For this task, I wanted to be able to create at least a strong melody that something more could be built on with additional time.

Outcome: At the end of the hour, I at least had the bones of a song that was somewhat presentable. I am a bit of a perfectionist when it comes to writing and producing music, so parts of what I completed within the hour are not something I would be fully happy to release to streaming services. I would prefer to go back over the music, chords, melody, instruments, and lyrics to see where I could improve it.

Reflection: This experiment showed that speed is not the same thing as artistry. While AI can produce music quickly, the human process still carries intention and individuality.
