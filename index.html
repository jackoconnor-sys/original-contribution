Generative AI and the Future of the Music Industry

This website has been created as an original contribution for a University College Cork project in the module CS5062: Computing in Society.

What will be shown on this website:

Through this website, I will present my own experiment of generative AI output, and also conduct another experiment to see what I personally can achieve in a given time frame without the use of generative AI. These will include examples, comparisons and reflections.

Why this subject matters:

As an independent artist, this topic is close to my heart. Generative AI is entering a music industry already shaped by low streaming income, and massive uncertainty around artistic value. Because of this, it is important to ask whether AI supports musicians, or further devalues their work, and the entire music industry as a whole.

I think that, given the tools of generative AI, there could be some value in how it is used. For example, it may be useful for educational purposes, for quickly finding instruments or sounds, or for helping people navigate music-making software. After carrying out these experiments, however, I am still of the belief that there is far more value in honing a craft, whether that be songwriting or music production, and in finding a community in which to collaborate with other artists. For the music industry as a whole, I believe generative AI may cause more damage than good, as artists are already bearing the brunt of low streaming income and the devaluation of music in general. At the same time, this may also force some kind of evolution, particularly in live performance, and raise questions such as: how can we get people to value the performance aspect of music again, rather than experiencing it only through phones and streaming services?
